Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, February 19, 2024

Nicole Ari Parker Biography

Nicole Ari Parker (Nicole Ari Parker-Kodjoe) is an American actress and model. Parker made her screen debut with a leading role in the critically acclaimed independent film The Incredibly True Adventure of Two Girls in Love in 1995 and later went to appear in Boogie Nights in 1997 directed by Paul Thomas Anderson.

Parker has starred in a number of movies, include Blue Streak in 1999, Remember the Titans in 2000, Brown Sugar in 2002, Welcome Home Roscoe Jenkins in 2008, Black Dynamite in 2009 and Almost Christmas in 2016. On television, Parker played the leading role of attorney Teri Joseph in the Showtime drama series Soul Food from 2000 to 2004), for which she received five NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Actress in a Drama Series nominations. Additionally, Parker starred in the short-lived UPN romantic comedy Second Time Around (2004-05) and ABC drama Time After Time in 2017. she joined the cast of Fox prime time soap opera Empire in 2017 playing Giselle Barker.

Nicole Ari Parker Age

Nicole Ari Parker was born on October 7, 1970 in Baltimore, Maryland, U.S. As of 2018 she is  48 years old.

Nicole Ari Parker Height

Parker stands at a height of 5 Feet 8 Inch.

Nicole Ari Parker Husband | Nicole Ari Parker And Boris Kodjoe

Nicole Ari Parker Parker married her Soul Food co-star Boris Kodjoe, on May 21, 2005, in Gundelfingen, Germany. Previously Parker was married to actor Joseph Falasca. Their marriage lasted only eight months and later that year they divorced.

Nicole Ari Parker Kids

In March 5, 2005, she gave birth to their first child, a girl. Sophie has spina bifida, which was diagnosed at birth. In October 2006, Parker gave birth to the couple’s second child, a son, in Atlanta, Georgia.

Nicole Ari Parker Family

Nicole Ari Parker is the only child of her divorced parents, Susan Parker a health care professional and Donald Parker a dentist.

Nicole Ari Parker Net Worth

Nicole has a net worth of $5 million. Much of this has been from her huge roles in films and TV shows throughout her 25-year acting career. Nicole’s role as an attorney on the show Soul Food (2000-2004) is among the most significant contributors to her fortune.

Nicole Ari Parker Religion

Parker is a christian, the family attends the Cascade United Methodist Church when in Atlanta.

Nicole Ari Parker Education

After briefly attending the Montessori School, she entered Roland Park Country School where she stayed through high school. Parker won Best Actress in the state of Maryland’s high school competition whe she was 17 years old, and then moved to The Washington Ballet Company before applying to the New York University’s Tisch School of the Arts. In 1993, Parker graduated with an acting degree.

Nicole Ari Parker Career

Early in her career Parker appeared in several critically acclaimed independent films including The Incredibly True Adventure of Two Girls in Love, 200 Cigarettes, Boogie Nights, and the 1999 Sundance Film Festival winner The Adventures of Sebastian Cole. She starred with Boris Kodjoe, her husband, model and actor, in UPN’s situation comedy Second Time Around.

After a string of odd jobs, bit roles and low wage work in the theater circuit, Parker was offered her breakthrough role on Soul Food. In Soul Food, Parker showcased her singing voice in several episodes. She also had a guest appearance on CSI: Crime Scene Investigation, playing a singer. In the movie Divas alongside Khalil Kain, Parker played the role of Stephanie in the movie Divas alongside Khalil Kain. Parker also had a lead role in the 1998 film Exiled: A Law & Order Movie. She worked alongside Martin Lawrence in two of his films: Welcome Home Roscoe Jenkins and Blue Streak. She also opened on Broadway in the play A Streetcar Named Desire, in the role of Blanche DuBois.

In the 2013, Parker was lead actress on NBC drama pilot, Secret Lives of Husbands and Wives and later had a recurring role on Revolution. Parker starred as a series regular in 2014, in the first season of the TNT crime drama Murder in the First, opposite Taye Diggs and Kathleen Robertson. Parker joined the cast of Fox prime time soap opera Empire in 2017, playing Giselle Barker, in a recurring basic during fourth season. Parker has been promoted to a series regular for the fifth season.
