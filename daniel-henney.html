Daniel Henney is an American actor, voice actor, singer, and model who is known for his television roles like playing Dr. Henry Kim in the romantic comedy-drama series My Lovely Sam Soon, Philip in the romantic melodrama series Spring Waltz, Dr. David Lee in the medical drama series Three Rivers, Kai in the action romantic comedy series The Fugitive: Plan B, Lan Mandragoran in the high fantasy series The Wheel of Time, Tadashi Hamada (voice) in the superhero animated series Big Hero 6: The Series, and Matthew “Matt” Simmons in the police procedural crime drama series Criminal Minds and Criminal Minds: Beyond Borders. Additionally, Daniel Henney has also been a part of a variety of films which include the likes of X-Men Origins: Wolverine, Papa, My Father, Shanghai Calling, Money, The Last Stand, Big Hero 6, The Spy: Undercover Operation, and Confidential Assignment 2: International.

    Daniel Henney Facts

  • While in high school, he was a pretty good basketball player and in his senior year, led his high school team to MHSAA Central region (CSAA) championship.
  • He started his modeling career in 2001, while he was still studying in college.
  • He has revealed in his interviews that one of the reasons behind pursuing an acting career in South Korea was that he wanted to track down his mother’s family.
  • He co-starred as Sam Chao (a Sino-American lawyer sent to work in Shanghai for three months) in the 2012 romantic comedy film Shanghai Calling and this role won him the award for “Outstanding Achievement in Acting” at the Newport Beach Film Festival and the award for “Best New Actor” at the Shanghai International Film Festival.
  • In his free time, he likes to strum his guitar and read.
  • He is a dog lover and has dogs named, Mango and Stella.
  • In 2021, he started appearing as Lan Mandragoran in the high fantasy streaming television series The Wheel of Time. His co-stars for the project include Rosamund Pike, Zoë Robins, Sophie Okonedo, Kae Alexander, and others.
  • He brought to life the character of Jack (an FBI agent) in the 2022 South Korean action comedy film Confidential Assignment 2: International, the sequel to the 2017 film Confidential Assignment. For this performance, Daniel Henney received the “Popular Star Award” at the Blue Dragon Film Awards in 2022.
